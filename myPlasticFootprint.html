The idea

An app that tracks the usage and recycling of plastic products. Thus, encourages people to consume less and recycle more.

INITIAL WIREFRAME
USER PERSONAS

Main target: Young people below 40, frequent mobile phone users.
Secondary target: People over 40.

RACHEL

23, freshly graduated, millennial, trendy, vegan, not into 9-5 jobs, currently traveling the world, loves Yoga poses, loves everything organic, loves hastags, has high environmental concerns, has 3 (very) active social media accounts, dream occupation: travel blogger/influencer.

RACHEL GOES GREEN

Rachel saw the app from an ad on Facebook. Being well aware of human's excessive use of plastic through social media, Rachel immediately downloaded the app. Rachel started by adding all plastic and recycled/environmental friendly products she was having and realised she's still made more of a negative impact.

Since then, Rachel's been determined to reduce her plastic footprint by not buying any single-use plastic product, and trying to use only eco-friendly ones.

For every goal met, Rachel shares her progress on social media and soon influenced many of her friends and followers to download and use the app.

RACHEL'S DAD

58, homemaker, former accountant, nerdy, loves gardening, has one social media account with 14 friends (12 of which are family members & relatives, 2 unknowns from random friend requests), interested in world news & politics.

DAD GOES GREEN

Rachel made Rachel's dad install the app. Rachel's dad is aware of the environmental issues from TV and newspapers but believed that the problem must be solved by the top people (i.e. governments and large corps).

Rachel's dad used the app reluctantly. App sends notifications of recycling facts and reminders frequently (not too frequent though, otherwise, dad'd be annoyed). Eventually, Rachel's dad has subconsciously felt obliged to use the app.

Rachel's dad does not always remember to track every item used/recycled. But is motivated enough to change some habits (cycling instead of driving, less plastic wrap, etc.).

RACHEL'S GRANNY

81, widowed, friendly, loving, caring, easy-going, talks to Siri sometimes, enjoys houseworks, been through hard times, (almost) never throws away stuff, loves the 'old days'.

GRANNY GOES GREEN(ER)

Rachel's granny has already been recycling just about everything. Rachel told granny that the app would reward her recycling efforts.

App's interface is intuitive, granny quickly learned to use it. Granny loves to do stuff, and takes it seriously.

Granny tracks every single item recycled, and soon get on top of the leaderboard with unbeatable records.
