The idea

This is an app that uses AI to assess symptoms just like a healthcare professional. It also acts as a doctor booking platform and personal healthcare assistance to monitor and track symptoms and treatments. The ambition of this platform is to educate the general public about health issues and connect them with healthcare professionals in a streamlined process. I believe that the more medical knowledge people have, the less likely they are to ignore or self-treat a symptom. Especially in developing countries where the healthcare systems are not yet so advanced.

Why and How it works

Why: a study found that 65% out of 2,000 polled people in the US googled their symptoms (LetsGetChecked's survey stated in phillyvoice's article). And that is only a small fraction of the population in the US alone. Self-diagnosis is definitely not encouraged, that is why people should and deserve to better understand their health issues.

How: Symptom checker is not a new concept with many webs/apps currently available in the market. However, their accuracy is questionable (sciencealert). Most of those symptom checkers work like a search engine that pairs the inputted symptom(s) with their matching conditions while failing to 'think' as a doctor. Schecker's algorithm assesses the symptoms the way a physician would. Its database contains all information of the conditions, the symptoms they cause, together with all surrounding factors that could lead to such symptoms. The AI analyses the user's inputs and info (medical history, gender, age, diets, etc.) to asks relevant/rational corresponding questions and seek for the link(s) between those and matching conditions to filters out results.
