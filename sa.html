Standards Australia

Standards Australia is a non-governmental, non-profit organisation. They were looking for a new website that looks visually appealing while being aligned with their brand guidelines.

Project type
Website re-design.

My Role
Visual design.

THE CHALLENGE

As an informative NGO website, content display and hiarachy were very important. The most challenging part was to optimise and scale mandatory contents so that they lie above the fold across multiple screen/viewport sizes as per client's requirements.

MULTI-TIER MENU

Menu had up to 3 levels, each must show a brief of info on hover. We had to discuss a solution that would meet the UX/UI desires while not exceeding the estimated developing time and efforts.

STANDARDS CATEGORY STYLING

We assigned each Standards Category a monochromatic set of coloured blocks for visual and filtering purposes.

SEARCH

We modernised the expanded search panel to be clear and bold showing popular/most searched contents and standards.

SEARCH RESULTS

Results displayed in visually distinguished Categories and were sortable/filterable.

MAIN INFO PAGES

The project required different layouts for most of the pages, so we came up with a solution to breakdown contents into modules/blocks instead of individual page design for flexibility, and for the sake of development time and effort.

FORM & POPUP STYLING

EDM STYLING

Project Conclusion

Despite we proposed the website to be designed base on a 12-col bootstrap grid system, lots of alterations were made as client had different requirements on margins, lengths and visibility of contents 'above the fold' and expected the display to retain the same across different screen dimensions. We also committed lots of responsive testings. Flexibility is the key, after all.
