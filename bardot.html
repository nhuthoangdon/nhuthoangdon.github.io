Bardot

As a big eCommerce website, the project had a lot to take into consideration. Our job was to optimise the whole site's UX/UI issues and deliver a refreshed one as part of their re-platform process.

Project type
Website re-design.

My Role
UX/UI design.

CHALLENGES

Many micro interactions, out-of-scope functionalities, heavy mobile optimisation.

SEARCH

The old search bar was visually unappealing and hard to find. We re-designed it with trending items as a pre-suggestion since we wanted to take any opportunities to introduce these. Psychologically speaking, we believed pre-suggestions would serve as 'hints' to draw user's attention and probably alter their search intentions.

SEARCH SUGGESTIONS

Taking into account the scenarios and keywords customers likely to search for in an eCommerce fashion website, we decided to display the suggestions not only base on the keywords but were also subjected to popularity/availability. Product tiles/images were also placed intentionally as suggestions, because images speak better than words (particularly true in the fashion world).

MINI LOGIN - CART - WISHLIST

Base on results and heatmaps from user research, the majority of user interactions were within the top. Login, Wishlish and Bag were the most clicked. We decluttered the old header by removing unnecessary items, separating those links from the main category menu and designed a new streamlined navigation with mandatory items easily accessible.

MEGA DROPDOWN MENU

We blew a new wind into the dropdown menu by integrating CTA banners (of trending items, promotions, collections, etc.) that were relevant to each category. We made use of the header's real estate as much as possible for highlight contents.

LOCATE IN-STORE

An out-of-scope function to show which stores the item is currently available at. We agreed that this should come up as a popup for a quick check-and-close action before moving on with the shopping.

STORE FINDER

A store listing page with integrated Store finder and filter. Store-related promotions could also be shown intentionally (if applicable).

PRODUCT BADGES

5 types of badges required. Each had to be visually distinguished from one another and fit well together within each product tile.
