Bardot

Bardot is one of the most iconic Australia's high fashion brands for women and juniors. I was in a UX/UI team to deliver a refreshed eCommerce website as part of their re-platform process.

Project type
Website re-design.

My Role
UX/UI design. Created for S60.

THE CHALLENGE

As a dedicated eCommerce website, the project required a lot of micro interactions and functionalities together with heavy mobile optimisation. My role was to take over the project after the initial concept round from the UX/UI practise lead, gather insights from user research team and roll out the designs within an allocated timeframe and budget. I also collaborated with a cross-functional team of developers, project managers, and testers to ensure the consistency between the live site and the design.

SEARCH

Search panel featured trending items as 'hints' for customers as we wanted to prioritise introducing and selling those.

SEARCH SUGGESTION

Search suggestions were subjected to keywords and items' availability/popularity, displayed base on the Categories taxonomy.

MINI LOGIN - CART - WISHLIST

Base on results and heatmaps from user research team, the majority of user interactions were within the top. Login, Wishlish and Bag were one of the most-clicked items. Thus header interactions were very important. We wanted to provide customers a hassle-free shopping experience, thus we decluttered their current header by removing unnecessary items and designed a new streamlined one with only mandatory items easily accessible.

MEGA DROPDOWN MENU

Dropdown navigation menu with integrated CTA banners suggesting trending items, promotions, collections, etc. that were relevant to each category. We made use of the header's real estate as much as possible for contents that we wanted to draw customers' attention to.

LOCATE IN-STORE

Client wanted to integrate a function to show which stores the item is currently available at for customers who decided to purchase the item in-store. We agreed that this should come up as a popup for a quick check-and-close action before moving on with the shopping.

STORE FINDER

Not to be confused with "Locate in-store". Store finder was basically a stores listing page.

PRODUCT BADGES

5 types of badges required. Each had to be visually distinguished from one another and fit well together within each product tile (maximum of 4 badges per product).

LEARNINGS

The project involved many stakeholders. There were quite an amount of change requests and out-of-scope additional features. Therefore, the communication had to be smooth, otherwise, the outcomes could be disastrous. Having been through similar projects facing the same problems, we had created a uniform communication process in which we made sure everyone was on track of even the slightest changes. Additionally, I gained more experience on compromising between involved parties to provide users with the best experience possible.
