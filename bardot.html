Bardot

Being one of the most iconic Australia's high fashion brands, Bardot's website had a lot to take into consideration. The old website was outdated with clunky header and ineffectively organised contents. Our job was to optimise and deliver a refreshed eCommerce website as part of their re-platform process.

Project type
Website re-design.

My Role
UX/UI design.

THE CHALLENGE

As a dedicated eCommerce website, the project required many micro interactions, functionalities and heavy mobile optimisation. We had to keep in mind all aspects during the design process, from design principles to user testings, to meeting project timeframe and budget, so on and so forth.

SEARCH

'Search' function is one important part of an eCommerce website. The old search bar was visually unappealing and hard to find. We re-designed it with trending items as a pre-suggestion because psychologically speaking, we thought they would serve as 'hints' to draw user's attention and would probably alter what they want to search for.

SEARCH SUGGESTIONS

Taking into account the scenarios and keywords customers likely to search for in an eCommerce fashion website, we decided to display the suggestions not only base on the keywords but were also subject to popularity/availability. Product tiles/images were also placed intentionally as suggestions, because after all, images speak better than words ( particularly true in the fashion world).

MINI LOGIN - CART - WISHLIST

Base on results and heatmaps from user research, the majority of user interactions were within the top. Login, Wishlish and Bag were the most clicked. We decluttered the old header by removing unnecessary items, separating those links from the main category menu and designed a new streamlined navigation with mandatory items easily accessible.

MEGA DROPDOWN MENU

We blew a new wind into the dropdown menu by integrating CTA banners (of trending items, promotions, collections, etc.) that were relevant to each category. We made use of the header's real estate as much as possible for highlight contents.

LOCATE IN-STORE

An out-of-scope function to show which stores the item is currently available at. We agreed that this should come up as a popup for a quick check-and-close action before moving on with the shopping.

STORE FINDER

A store listing page with integrated Store finder and filter. Store-related promotions could also be shown intentionally (if applicable).

PRODUCT BADGES

5 types of badges required. Each had to be visually distinguished from one another and fit well together within each product tile.

Project Conclusion

The project involved many stakeholders. There were quite an amount of change requests and out-of-scope features. Therefore, the communication had to be smooth, otherwise, the outcomes could be disastrous. Having been through similar projects facing the same problems, we had created a uniform communication process in which we made sure everyone was on track of even a slight change. Lots of compromise was also made between different teams and stakeholders.
