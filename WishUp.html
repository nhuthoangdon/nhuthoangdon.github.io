The idea

A social network for the community to help fulfillling lifetime wishes of the elderly, and those in special circumtances.

Why?

I believe everyone (well, most of everyone) has at least a lifetime wish, yet not all was/is fortunate enough to fulfill it while they still have the time and assets to.

REMEMBER THAT MOVIE UP?

It's the movie in which Carl - an elderly widowed man, tries to fulfill his late wife - Ellie's lifetime wish of getting to the Paradise Falls against all odds. He gets there in a balloons floating house with helps from a boy, a dog and a Dodo bird.

There must be many Carls and Ellies in real life, but without balloons floating houses and such special friends. So this social network serves as an asset for the community to help the real life Carls fulfilling their lifetime wishes - with kindness and compassion.

A 'CARL' STORY

Imagin if Carl was in real life, and Russel would like to help him after knowing his situation. Russel would use the app to post Carl's story so everyone could give a hand.

HOW DO PEOPLE HELP?

Main actions that the audience can do to help Carl are: Wish luck to Carl, Donate, and Offer Carl something. And of course there are the sharing and commenting ones as well.

'Wish luck' has the same concept as the 'like' on mainstream social media; 'Donate' is where people can quickly make a donation so that the funds will be used to help Carl achieving his lifetime wish; 'Offer' is for someone who would like to support Carl another way (e.g. accommodations, companionships, etc.).
